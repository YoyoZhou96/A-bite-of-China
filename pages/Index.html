A Bite of China III



"The Third Season of China on the Bite of the Tongue" is a documentary produced by CCTV, produced by CCTV Documentary Channel, and directed by Liu Hongyan.


The documentary is divided into eight episodes: "Qi", "Scent", "Banquet", "Nurturing", "Cooking Man", "Crisp", "Four Seasons", and "Integration", telling the warmth story behind people and food.


Spring ploughing, summer harvest, autumn harvest, winter storage, the oriental philosophy of harmony between man and nature allows Chinese food to change with time, wisdom and agility. The theory of nutrition and health in Chinese medicine has created a new world for the use of ingredients. Confucian ethics and morals give the heart and the taste of home. Our dining table. Washing history, blending time, generation after generation of Chinese raised fireworks between the heavens and the earth, cooking food with the utmost sincerity, between meals, the Chinese showed their individuality, confirmed their belonging, built civilization, understood harmony Grasp the mystery of the world. Chinese food is grown in the fertile soil of traditional culture, and in a wide time and space, with a heart of gratitude, we understand that food gives us precious nourishment

Work Evaluation


With the popularity of the "Water Eight Immortals" in the south of the Yangtze River, eight aquatic products of water chestnuts, lotus roots, wild rice, water cress, and water chestnuts plus cypress, water chestnuts, and gorgon have been cooked as early as the spring and autumn. It is a health treasure with a unique flavor. The folklore says that "water chestnuts in spring, lotus root in summer, celery in late autumn, wild water chestnuts from March to October, and vegetables in aquatic seasons." The seasonal character produced in these four seasons is deeply loved by diners. "The Third Season of China on the Bite of the Tongue" made a very fresh presentation of "Water Eight Immortals". It not only introduced the method, but also introduced its origin, making people taste and think about the origin.
